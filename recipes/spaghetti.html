Equipment

  • stick blender and jug
  • large frying pan
  • large saucepan with lid
  • spatula
  • air fryer
  • tongs
  • scales
  • thermometer

Ingredients

  • jar of tomato pasta sauce
  • spaghetti, 75g per person
  • meatballs, 6 per person
  • salt (about 5g fine table salt depending on how much pasta and water)
  • parmesan (optional)
  • pepper (optional)

Method

  1. Get all equipment and ingredients ready.
  2. Optional: Blend sauce until smooth, covering with kitchen roll to prevent splatter.
  3. Pour sauce into saucepan and add the lid. Heat sauce over medium-high (about 5), stirring occasionally with spatula to prevent burning.
  4. Using scales, weight out spaghetti and add to frying pan with some salt.
  5. Boil kettle and pour over spaghetti until covered.
  6. Heat spaghetti at medium-high heat (about 7) to bring to boil.
  7. When the water starts boiling stir the pasta to avoid sticking. You can then lower the hob temperature a bit (to 5).
  8. Set air fryer to maximum temperature 205°C and set a 7 minute timer.
  9. Once preheated add meatballs to air fryer, shaking halfway through.
  10. Periodically taste the pasta until it is done. You want it to be soft and not stick in your teeth when bitten. Once cooked, add pasta to sauce.
  11. After 7 minutes, using the thermometer measure the temperature at the centre of a few meatballs. If majority are at 165 they are done, otherwise cook an extra minute until they are.
  12. When meatballs are cooked at them to the sauce.
  13. Mix together and serve, adding parmesan and pepper if desired.

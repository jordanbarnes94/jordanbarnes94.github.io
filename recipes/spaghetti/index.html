Spaghetti and Meatballs

A classic spaghetti and meatballs recipe with air-fried meatballs and smooth tomato sauce

ServingsVariable (75g pasta per person, 6 meatballs per person)
Prep Time10 minutes
Cook Time20 minutes
DifficultyEasy
pastameatballsitalianair-fryer

🔧 Equipment

  • • stick blender and jug
  • • large frying pan
  • • large saucepan with lid
  • • spatula
  • • air fryer
  • • tongs
  • • scales
  • • thermometer

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 jar
    tomato pasta sauce
  • 75g per person
    spaghetti
  • 6 per person
    meatballs
  • about 5g
    saltfine table salt depending on how much pasta and water
  • to taste
    parmesanoptional
  • to taste
    pepperoptional

👨‍🍳 Method

  1. 1
    Get all equipment and ingredients ready.
  2. 2
    Optional: Blend sauce until smooth, covering with kitchen roll to prevent splatter.
  3. 3
    Pour sauce into saucepan and add the lid. Heat sauce over medium-high (about 5), stirring occasionally with spatula to prevent burning.
  4. 4
    Using scales, weight out spaghetti and add to frying pan with some salt.
  5. 5
    Boil kettle and pour over spaghetti until covered.
  6. 6
    Heat spaghetti at medium-high heat (about 7) to bring to boil.
  7. 7
    When the water starts boiling stir the pasta to avoid sticking. You can then lower the hob temperature a bit (to 5).
  8. 8
    Set air fryer to maximum temperature 205°C and set a 7 minute timer.
  9. 9
    Once preheated add meatballs to air fryer, shaking halfway through.
  10. 10
    Periodically taste the pasta until it is done. You want it to be soft and not stick in your teeth when bitten. Once cooked, add pasta to sauce.
  11. 11
    After 7 minutes, using the thermometer measure the temperature at the centre of a few meatballs. If majority are at 165 they are done, otherwise cook an extra minute until they are.
  12. 12
    When meatballs are cooked add them to the sauce.
  13. 13
    Mix together and serve, adding parmesan and pepper if desired.

📝 Notes

  • 💡 Meatballs should reach internal temperature of 165°F/74°C
  • 💡 Pasta cooking time varies - taste test for doneness
  • 💡 Blending the sauce is optional but creates smoother texture
